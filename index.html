Abstract

CORGI reconstructs high-fidelity 3D dog assets from a single in-the-wild image without relying on paired 3D supervision. The framework first turns an arbitrary input pose into a canonical 360-degree pseudo multi-view sequence, then optimizes a consistency-aware deformable 3D Gaussian representation anchored by a D-SMAL dog prior, and finally repairs view-dependent artifacts with a self-supervised generative module.

The resulting models are designed to preserve breed-level appearance, reduce cross-view generative inconsistencies, and support realistic animation. DCGR
